Explanations for HW1 Part 4

I chose the wikiHow article "How to Survive in the Woods" and translated selected survival tasks into PDDL.
The PDDL domain is called survive_in_the_woods. It represents a small survival-planning world with players, 
locations, directions, items, and water.

The domain.pddl file defines the general rules of the world. 
The types include player, location, direction, item, and water. 
The predicates describe facts that can be true in the world, such as where objects are located, 
whether a player has an item, 
whether a location has a water source, 
whether water is untreated or treated, 
whether a fire exists, and 
whether the player has shelter.

The action schemas describe what the planner is allowed to do. 
For example, go moves the player between connected locations, get picks up an item, 
get_water collects water from a location with a water source, strain_water uses cloth to strain water, 
boil_water makes water treated if the player has a fire and a container, 
and build_shelter makes the player sheltered after the player has branches and leaves.

I undersood the three problem files.
- problem-collect-water.pddl asks the planner to get water into the player's inventory.
  The player starts at camp, picks up a canteen, walks to the waterfall, and collects water. 
- problem-purify-water.pddl asks the planner to make water treated. 
  The player starts with untreated water and uses cloth, a pot, kindling, wood, a fire pit, and fire to boil the water. 
- problem-build-shelter.pddl asks the planner to make the player sheltered by moving to the grove, 
  collecting branches and leaves, and building a shelter.

The JSON file survive_in_the_woods.json contains annotations connecting the PDDL elements to natural language descriptions 
from the wikiHow article. It includes the article URL and title, lists the PDDL types, actions, 
and predicates, and gives descriptions and mentions for selected actions and predicates. 
For example, boil_water is connected to the article instruction about boiling water, 
and has_water_source is connected to phrases about finding fresh water.

The main thing I learned is that PDDL is good for representing clear symbolic actions with preconditions and effects, 
but it is limited for real-world instructions. wikiHow often includes uncertainty, time, quantities, safety advice, 
and judgment calls. Those ideas are harder to represent in the simple STRIPS-style PDDL used in this assignment, 
so I simplified them into discrete predicates and actions.
